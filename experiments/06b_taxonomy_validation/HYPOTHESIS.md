# Hypothesis

Experienced developers will recognise the three agentic smell categories
(structural, semantic, intent) from their own recent work when presented
without prior explanation. Furthermore, having the term "agentic smell"
available changes how developers describe and escalate quality problems
in agent-assisted codebases.

## Prediction

- Recognition rate: a majority of experienced developers will provide
  specific, unprompted examples from their own work for at least two of
  the three categories.
- Nomenclature effect: developers given the taxonomy will describe
  quality problems with greater specificity and attribute them to
  process factors rather than model capability, compared to a control
  group without the terminology.

## Design Summary

Part 1: Present the three agentic smell categories to developers without
explanation. Measure recognition rate and specificity of self-reported
examples. Part 2: Compare how developers with and without the taxonomy
describe quality problems in agent-assisted development (between-subjects).
